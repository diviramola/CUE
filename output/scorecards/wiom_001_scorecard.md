## Dimension Details

### Hook: 3/5
The ad opens with a production title card, then cuts to a young woman on her phone. The first compelling visual (mother talking animatedly) arrives at ~10 seconds. For feed platforms, this is 7 seconds too late. No curiosity gap, no visual surprise, no scroll-stopping moment.

**Pattern match:** Partially C1 (no brand-first). Fails C2 (no curiosity engine).
**Reference:** Study ad_010 (Adani) for mystery-driven hook.

### Retention: 3/5
49-second narrative with consistent conversational pacing. No tonal shift, no energy spike. The auto driver enters at ~35s (71% mark) -- too late for a pattern interrupt (C4 says 40-60%). The duration is in the dead zone: too long for feed (30s), not earned for long-form (60s).

**Pattern match:** Partial C2 (mild open loop from driver's question). Fails C3 (duration) and C4 (late interrupt).
**Reference:** Study ad_007 (Cadbury) for earned 60s narrative.

### CTR Drivers: 2/5 (CRITICAL GAP)
100% dialogue-driven. Zero text overlays until end card. Zero sound-off survivability. On Meta where 85% of videos play without sound, this ad communicates nothing -- viewers see people talking near an auto-rickshaw with no context.

**Pattern match:** Partial K1 (narrative-connected product reveal). Complete fail on K2 (sound-off).
**Reference:** Study ad_002 (Lahori Zeera) for visual-first storytelling.

### CTA/Conversion: 2/5 (CRITICAL GAP)
The end card shows a tagline but no actual call to action. No download prompt, no app mention, no free trial, no QR code. The ad builds understanding and desire but gives the viewer nowhere to go.

**Pattern match:** Tagline is narrative-earned (K1). But a tagline is not a CTA.
**Reference:** Study ad_009 (Sony LIV) for bold creative + clear CTA combo.

### Brand Coherence: 4/5
The setting, casting, and dialect perfectly mirror Wiom's target audience. The auto-meter analogy is culturally resonant and distinctive. Brand appears only at end (correct strategy). Minor gap: text-only end card lacks a memorable brand moment.

## The Bottom Line

GAON is a **good TV ad trapped in a feed-first world**. Its core creative assets -- the auto-meter analogy, the authentic Tier 2-3 setting, the family dynamics -- are genuinely strong and should be preserved. But the ad was designed for sound-on, lean-back viewing (TV/YouTube pre-roll) and will underperform dramatically on Meta/Instagram feed where most of Wiom's audience encounters it.

The fixes are packaging, not substance: re-edit the hook, add text overlays for sound-off, create a 30s feed cut, and add a real CTA. The creative insight is already there -- it just needs the right delivery vehicle.
