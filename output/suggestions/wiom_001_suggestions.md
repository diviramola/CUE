# Improvement Suggestions: Wiom GAON (wiom_001)

**Current Score: 55/100**
**5 suggestions | 2 high priority | 2 medium | 1 low**

---

## What to Keep (Do NOT Change)

These elements are strong and must survive any re-edit:

- **The auto-meter analogy** -- Wiom's single most powerful creative asset. Makes metered broadband instantly understandable.
- **Authentic Tier 2-3 setting and casting** -- small-town street, auto-rickshaw, school uniform kid, blue saree mother. Real India, not aspirational India.
- **Pure Hindi dialogue in authentic small-town register** -- not Hinglish, not urban. Exactly right for the target audience.
- **TVF production quality** -- high craft that doesn't feel corporate.
- **Family as protagonists (4-5 members)** -- the exact household making broadband decisions in Tier 2-3 India.

---

## Suggestion 1: Add Sound-Off Text Overlays (HIGH PRIORITY)

**Dimension:** CTR Drivers | **Affects:** CTR | **Confidence:** High

**Current state:** The ad is 100% dialogue-driven with no text overlays. On Meta feed (85% sound-off), the viewer sees people talking near an auto-rickshaw with no context.

**What the playbook says (K2):** Ads designed to communicate their core message visually without audio significantly outperform on Meta feed.

**Suggested change:** Add 3-4 bold Hindi text overlays at key narrative beats:
1. "Bijli band, akhbaar band, doodh band..." (cancellation montage)
2. "Ghar ka net bhi band?" (when the driver asks)
3. "Jitna use karo, utna bharo" (the punchline)
4. Wiom CTA (end card)

Use large, high-contrast text readable on a phone screen. This preserves the narrative while making it work sound-off.

**Study:** ad_002 (Lahori Zeera) -- visual storytelling that works entirely without sound.

---

## Suggestion 2: Add a Real CTA (HIGH PRIORITY)

**Dimension:** CTA/Conversion | **Affects:** Downloads | **Confidence:** High

**Current state:** The ad ends with a tagline but no actual call to action. No app download prompt, no website, no QR code, no free trial offer.

**What the playbook says (K1):** The CTA should feel like the natural conclusion of the narrative. 12 of 13 top ads place a late CTA that connects to the story. But it must be a CTA, not just a tagline.

**Suggested change:** After the tagline, add a 3-second end card:
> "Download Wiom -- Pehla mahina free" (First month free)
> + App store badges + QR code

The "free trial" extends the meter metaphor -- try before you pay, just like an auto ride.

**Study:** ad_009 (Sony LIV) -- bold creative that still ends with a clear CTA (register now).

---

## Suggestion 3: Re-Edit the Hook (HIGH PRIORITY)

**Dimension:** Hook | **Affects:** Video Completion | **Confidence:** Medium

**Current state:** Opens with a production title card ("OFFLINE SEC") followed by a young woman on her phone. The first interesting visual arrives at ~10 seconds.

**What the playbook says (C1 + C2):** Open with a moment the viewer recognizes -- relatable problem, visual surprise, or curiosity gap. Create an unanswered question in the first 2-3 seconds.

**Suggested change:** Re-edit to open with the mother's animated dialogue about cancelling services:
> "Bijli band karo, akhbaar band karo, doodh band karo... aur haan, net bhi band karo!"

The rapid-fire cancellations create curiosity (why is everything being cancelled?) and "net bhi band karo" is the hook that sets up the rest. Cut the title card entirely for feed versions.

**Study:** ad_010 (Adani) -- opens with a mysterious catchphrase that creates instant curiosity.

---

## Suggestion 4: Create a 30-Second Feed Cut (MEDIUM PRIORITY)

**Dimension:** Retention | **Affects:** Video Completion | **Confidence:** Medium

**Current state:** 49 seconds with consistent conversational pacing. No tonal shift, no energy spike. The duration doesn't justify itself for feed contexts.

**What the playbook says (C3 + C4):** 30s is the sweet spot for digital. Place a pattern interrupt at 40-60% of the ad's duration.

**Suggested change:** Create a 30-second feed cut:
- 0-5s: Cancellation montage hook
- 5-15s: Auto-rickshaw ride + driver's curiosity
- 15-22s: Meter analogy explanation (energy shift = pattern interrupt at 50%)
- 22-27s: Family reaction + warmth beat
- 27-30s: Wiom tagline + CTA

Keep the 49s version for YouTube pre-roll where sound-on and longer attention are the norm.

**Study:** ad_003 (WD-40) -- tells complete problem-solution-payoff stories in 6 seconds.

---

## Suggestion 5: Design a Wiom Brand Moment (LOW PRIORITY)

**Dimension:** Brand Coherence | **Affects:** Downloads | **Confidence:** Low

**Current state:** Wiom appears only in a text-only end card. No logo animation, no audio sting, no recurring visual motif.

**Suggested change:** Design a 3-second Wiom brand moment: the auto-meter visual metaphor as a branded animation (meter ticking = data being used), accompanied by a short audio signature. Use this consistently across all GAON campaign ads. The meter-as-logo concept reinforces the USP visually.

**Study:** ad_012 (Surf Excel) -- "Daag Acche Hain" has built a brand identity so strong the tagline itself is cultural.

---

## Coherence Check

The suggestions focus on **packaging** (hook, sound-off, CTA, duration) rather than **substance**. The ad's core -- the auto-meter analogy, the family setting, the cultural register -- is strong and must not change.

**Risk:** Adding text overlays could make it feel cluttered. A 30s cut could lose the warmth of the longer narrative.

**Recommendation:** Create the 30s feed cut AND keep the 49s version for YouTube pre-roll. Test both.
